How do we start working with you?

The path is clear: from your first message or call until materials arrive on site. These are the steps we follow with consultants, contractors, and developers.

1
Receiving project drawings and key data
We start by receiving architectural and structural drawings, door and window schedules, and any consultant specifications related to wood, aluminum, and glass.
2
Technical review and clarifying grey areas
Our team reviews the technical details, identifies any gaps or conflicts in specifications, then shares questions and notes with you to ensure clarity before pricing or supply.
3
Proposing supply solutions and quantity schedules
We translate project requirements into practical supply solutions: profile systems, glass thicknesses, wood types, and finishes – with preliminary BOQs that make costs and alternatives easier to understand.
4
Approving samples and colors with owner or consultant
We prepare real samples for colors and finishes and support you in the approval process with the owner and consultant until a final agreed specification is reached for supply and execution.
5
Scheduling deliveries and coordination with site
Once details are agreed, we set a supply plan linked to the project schedule and coordinate with the site team to ensure materials arrive at the right time and with the approved specs.

How our solutions support different project types

Each service line has its own way of enhancing project performance. Here’s a closer look at how aluminum, glass, and wood are used in real commercial and residential buildings.

Façades and aluminum systems for modern projects

In commercial and high-rise façades, aluminum is what ties glass, insulation, and the final visual identity together. We help you select systems that balance aesthetics and technical performance.

Adapting profiles to façade height and wind loads.
Single or double glazing options according to insulation needs.
Ability to integrate façade systems with automatic doors and main entrances.
Ideal for malls, towers, and office buildings
How does this line help you?
• Reduces coordination time between consultant and contractor on façade details.
• Achieves a façade that visually matches the architectural concept.
• Improves long-term thermal and acoustic performance of the envelope.

Wood finishes as a tool to elevate interior value

Wood does more than add warmth; it raises the perceived quality in corridors, receptions, and meeting areas. We support you in selecting materials that handle real use and stay consistent in color over time.

Choosing board thicknesses and types suitable for traffic levels.
Considering grain direction and its impact on spatial perception.
Matching colors with doors, floors, and other project elements.
Ideal for hotels, offices, and high-end residential projects
Added value for design teams
• A clear reference for wood tones and patterns used in the project.
• Easier final sample approval with owners and developers.
• Less variation between batches in long-running projects.

Glass as both protection and a design feature

From external façades to balustrades and internal partitions, we provide safety glass solutions that respect safety codes, user privacy, and the desired daylight profile inside the space.

Laminated or tempered glass options according to safety requirements.
Controlling transparency and reflection to maintain privacy without full blackout.
Integrating glass with aluminum or wood in the same architectural details.
Ideal for façades, stairs, and interior partitions
What does this mean on site?
• Fewer last-minute changes due to glass type being switched late in the project.
• Clear definition of glass thickness and build-up for each area.
• Better compatibility with the aluminum systems used in the project.
